Error Chart > Overview

Error charts are conventional charts with a special ability to show the extent of uncertainty in information relative to its true value. With the help of its distinct I-shaped bars, these charts indicate the range of possible errors or deviations(both positive and negative) that might occur in data. Typically these charts represent the standard deviation in a measurement indicating the amount of differences that reside in a set of data. Error charts give a general idea of the accuracy of information or conversely the amount of variation in data from its original or expected value.

Following are the areas where these types of charts are used:

  • Appliance Testing
  • Scientific Research
  • Monitoring Systems
  • Weather Analysis
  • Human Resource Management

At present, there are three types of multi-series error charts available in the PowerCharts v3 suite:

  1. Error Bar chart
  2. Error Line chart
  3. Error Scatter chart (XY Plot)

Features of Error charts:

  • Supports both half and full error bars for positive and negative errors respectively.
  • Options to configure the error bars by changing its color, thickness and alpha.
  • Customizable width of error bars depending on the importance of deviation from the data itself.
